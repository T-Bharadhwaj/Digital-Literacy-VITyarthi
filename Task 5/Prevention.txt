Prevention Guide: Stay safe from cybercrime
To protect yourself from cybercrimes like UPI fraud, ransomware, it is essential to follow safe online practices.
Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all university portals, email accounts, and social media to add an extra layer of security beyond passwords.
Use strong, unique passwords for every account; consider using a trusted password manager to keep track of them safely.
Verify UPI payment requests before entering your PIN. Remember: You only need to enter your UPI PIN to SEND money, never to receive it.
Set daily UPI transaction limits and enable 'SMS alerts' for all banking transactions to quickly spot any unauthorized activity.
Avoid using public Wi-Fi in cafes or libraries for sensitive tasks like online banking or entering personal details; use a VPN or mobile data instead.
Be cautious of 'Job Offer' or 'Scholarship' scams on WhatsApp/Telegram that ask for an upfront 'processing fee' or sensitive documents.
Regularly update your smartphone's OS and apps to ensure you have the latest security patches against known vulnerabilities.
Think before you click on shortened links in SMS (Smishing) or emails that claim your account is blocked or offer 'free' rewards.
Reporting: Immediately report any cyber fraud or harassment at the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call the Helpline 1930.
